I am a senior undergraduate student at the University of Connecticut majoring in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Mathematics. My research interests are fluid mechanics, optimization, nonlinear stability analysis, and computational fluid dynamics, with a current focus on transient growth, input-output amplification, and Lyapunov-based analysis of wall-bounded shear flows.

I am an undergraduate researcher in the FLUids, rEduction, Nonlinearity, and Turbulence Lab at UConn, where I work with Professor Chang Liu on nonlinear and time-varying flow stability problems. In Fall 2026, I will pursue my PhD at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, advised by Professor Lin Fu.

You can also find my work on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=BqbfWaYAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN) and [ORCID](https://orcid.org/0009-0006-9610-3996).
